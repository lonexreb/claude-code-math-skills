**Definition 7.1.11.** If ( _𝑋, 𝑑_ ) is a metric space, _𝑌_ ⊂ _𝑋_ , and _𝑑_[′] � _𝑑_ | _𝑌_ × _𝑌_ , then ( _𝑌, 𝑑_[′] ) is said to be a _subspace_ of ( _𝑋, 𝑑_ ).

It is common to simply write _𝑑_ for the metric on _𝑌_ , as it is the restriction of the metric on _𝑋_ . Sometimes we say _𝑑_[′] is the _subspace metric_ and _𝑌_ has the _subspace topology_ .

A subset of the real numbers is bounded whenever all its elements are at most some fixed distance from 0. When dealing with an arbitrary metric space there may not be some natural fixed point 0, but for the purposes of boundedness it does not matter.
