Example 1.1.10

**Example 1.1.10:** The reader may have heard about the _complex numbers_ , usually denoted by ℂ. That is, ℂ is the set of numbers of the form _𝑥_ + _𝑖𝑦_ , where _𝑥_ and _𝑦_ are real numbers, and _𝑖_ is the imaginary number, a number such that _𝑖_[2] = −1. The reader may remember from algebra that ℂ is also a field; however, it is not an ordered field. While one can make ℂ into an ordered set in some way, it is not possible to put an order on ℂ that would make it an ordered field: In every ordered field, −1 _<_ 0 and _𝑥_[2] _>_ 0 for all nonzero _𝑥_ , but in ℂ, _𝑖_[2] = −1.

Finally, an ordered field that has the least-upper-bound property has the corresponding property for greatest lower bounds.
