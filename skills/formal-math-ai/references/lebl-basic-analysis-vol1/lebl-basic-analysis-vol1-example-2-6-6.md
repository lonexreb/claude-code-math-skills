A power series is really a function of _𝑥_ , and many important functions in analysis can be written as a power series. We use the convention that 0[0] = 1 (that is, if _𝑥_ = _𝑥_ 0 and _𝑛_ = 0).

A power series is said to be _convergent_ if there is at least one _𝑥_ ≠ _𝑥_ 0 that makes the series converge. If _𝑥_ = _𝑥_ 0, then the series always converges since all terms except the first are zero. If the series does not converge for any point _𝑥_ ≠ _𝑥_ 0, we say that the series is _divergent_ .
