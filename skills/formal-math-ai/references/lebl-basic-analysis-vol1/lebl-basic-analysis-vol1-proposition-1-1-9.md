Proposition 1.1.9

**Proposition 1.1.9.** _Let 𝑥, 𝑦_ ∈ _𝐹, where 𝐹 is an ordered field. If 𝑥𝑦 >_ 0 _, then either both 𝑥 and 𝑦 are positive, or both are negative._

_Proof._ We show the contrapositive: If either one of _𝑥_ or _𝑦_ is zero, or if _𝑥_ and _𝑦_ have opposite signs, then _𝑥𝑦_ is not positive. If _𝑥_ or _𝑦_ is zero, then _𝑥𝑦_ is zero and hence not positive. Hence assume that _𝑥_ and _𝑦_ are nonzero and have opposite signs. Without loss of generality, suppose _𝑥 >_ 0 and _𝑦 <_ 0. Multiply _𝑦 <_ 0 by _𝑥_ to get _𝑥𝑦 <_ 0 _𝑥_ = 0. □
