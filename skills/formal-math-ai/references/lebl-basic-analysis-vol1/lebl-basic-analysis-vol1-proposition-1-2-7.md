Proposition 1.2.7

**Proposition 1.2.7.** _Let 𝐴, 𝐵_ ⊂ ℝ _be nonempty sets such that 𝑥_ ≤ _𝑦 whenever 𝑥_ ∈ _𝐴 and 𝑦_ ∈ _𝐵. Then 𝐴 is bounded above, 𝐵 is bounded below, and_ sup _𝐴_ ≤ inf _𝐵._

_Proof._ Any _𝑥_ ∈ _𝐴_ is a lower bound for _𝐵_ . Therefore, _𝑥_ ≤ inf _𝐵_ for all _𝑥_ ∈ _𝐴_ , so inf _𝐵_ is an upper bound for _𝐴_ . Hence, sup _𝐴_ ≤ inf _𝐵_ . □
